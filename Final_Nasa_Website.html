Sunny's Journey

A digital story about space weather and its impact on our lives

The Sun gives us warmth and light, but sometimes it sends Earth something unexpected—storms of energy we call space weather.

What Is Space Weather?

Although the Sun is 93 million miles away, it’s closely connected to life on Earth. Most of the time, the Sun shines calmly. But sometimes, it explodes with power—releasing solar flares and coronal mass ejectio (CMEs).

These massive bursts of energy and particles travel through space. When they reach Earth, we call it space weather.

How Does Space Weather Affect Us?

  • 🌾 Farmers – GPS signals that guide tractors and track crops can fail during solar storms.
  • ✈️ Pilots – Airplanes flying near the poles sometimes lose radio contact when solar flares hit.
  • 👩‍🚀 Astronauts – Solar radiation puts astronauts at risk, so missions need extra protection.
  • ⚡ Power Grid Operators – Strong solar storms can overload power grids, leading to blackouts.
  • 🌌 General Public – Solar storms also create auroras, the beautiful northern and southern lights.

Why Should We Care?

Space weather is invisible, but its effects are real. A single strong solar storm can disrupt satellites, communication, electricity, and daily life on Earth.

That’s why scientists around the world monitor the Sun every day. By studying solar activity, they can predict storms, protect astronauts, safeguard power grids, and prepare us for the next big event.

Understanding space weather means understanding how connected we are to the Sun—even from 93 million miles away.
